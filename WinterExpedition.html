Winter Spiti Expedition

The Winter Spiti Expedition promises an extraordinary journey through the spellbinding landscapes of the Spiti Valley, situated in the trans-Himalayan region of Himachal Pradesh, India. Known for its rugged terrain and remote location, Spiti transforms into a winter wonderland during the colder months, offering a unique and challenging adventure for intrepid travelers. This expedition takes enthusiasts on a thrilling odyssey through snow-covered valleys, pristine monasteries, and ancient villages, showcasing the raw beauty of the Himalayas.
The Winter Spiti Expedition is not for the faint-hearted, as it involves navigating through snow-laden roads, exploring frozen lakes, and braving the sub-zero temperatures that envelop the region. Key highlights include visits to iconic monasteries like Key and Tabo, known for their ancient murals and spiritual significance. The journey also provides an opportunity to witness the traditional lifestyle of the locals in Spiti, who endure harsh winter conditions with resilience and warmth.
Beyond the physical challenges, the Winter Spiti Expedition offers a unique perspective on the region's cultural and natural treasures, making it an unforgettable experience for those seeking an offbeat adventure. The stark beauty of the snow-clad landscapes, coupled with the tranquility of the winter season, creates a magical atmosphere that sets this expedition apart as a once-in-a-lifetime exploration of the Himalayan frontier.

Winter Spiti Expedition

Overview

Spiti Valley is a cold desert mountain valley located high in the Himalayas in the north-eastern part of the Himachal Pradesh. The name “Spiti” means “The Middle Land” , i.e. the land between Tibet and India. Winters in Spiti are extremely harsh and temperatures sometimes fall to -20º Celsius. Winters here are frigid and there is no running water in taps. There is an endless thrill in SPITI VALLEY WINTER EXPEDITION.

Quick Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival to Shimla and Pickup from there to Kalpa

Today, when you arrive at Shimla Bus Station, our driver will meet you and your journey to Kalpa will begin. Enjoy the scenic views of Shimla, Kufri, Tranda Dhank, and the lovely villages of Rampur Wangtu and Karcham along the way. Visit Roghi Village and Suicide Point upon arrival in Kalpa. You are free to visit and explore the Peo Market on your own. View the famous Kinner Kailash mountain range from Kalpa. Check into the Stay and call it a day. Later, enjoy dinner and an overnight stay at Kalpa.
(NOTE: The vehicle will only pick up all passengers from one location. As soon as you leave, you will arrive at your next destination).
Distance Covered: 220-250 Kms Travel Time: 08-09 hrs Cab Meal: Dinner

DAY 2: Kalpa - Nako - Tabo

Today, after breakfast, we drive to Tabo. On the way, stop at Khab for a breathtaking view of the Satluj and Spiti rivers. You can eat lunch in Nako village, the first village in the Spiti valley region and famous for its beautiful lake. Visit Gue village on the way to see the Mummy (Depends upon the road condition). Later, the journey to Tabo was resumed. On Arrival, Check-In to the Stay & Retire for the Day. Dinner and Overnight stay at Tabo. Gue Mummy- Believed to over 500 years old, this rare natural mummy belongs to Sangha Tenzin, a Buddhist monk who started the selfmummification process while still living! In 1975, an earthquake in the windsculpted Spiti Valley opened up a time-worn tomb in Gue. Tabo, Is a small town in Lahaul and Spiti district on the banks of the Spiti River. The town lies on the road between Reckong Peo and Kaza. Visit the Tabo village, surrounded by Buddhist Monasteries which is more than 1000 years old and is considered one of the holiest Buddhist sites in the world. There are several caves nearby which are used by Buddhist monks for meditation in solitude. (NOTE:- Please note Road Conditions to - Gue Village are not good. If at the time of the travel roads are closed or not in good condition then will not cover the same. It’s for Guest Safety Only). Distance Covered: 188-200 Kms Travel Time: 06-07 hrs Cab Meal: Breakfast and Dinner

DAY 3: Tabo - Dhankar - Pin Valley - Kaza

Today morning after breakfast Visit - Tabo Monastery and Caves. Later on Start your Journey for Kaza Via Dhankar. Visit - Dhankar village and visit Gompa which is situated high above the valley. Later on Visit Pin Valley. Explore village life and spend some time in Kungri monastery. Later on move towards Kaza (Lidang). On Arrival at Kaza (Lidang), Check-In to the Stay and retire for the day. Dinner and ovenight stay at Kaza (Lidang). Dhankar Village, Is known for a fort and residence of the king of Spiti. The views from this point are mind blowing. Pin Valley, Is famous for its wildlife which includes critically endangered snow leopards, Siberian ibex, Himalayan bear and wolf. Kaza, Is the sub divisional headquarters of the remote Spiti Valley and known as Heart of Spiti. Kaza is a high altitude or cold desert having close similarities to the neighbouring Tibet and Ladakhregions in terms of terrain, climate and the Buddhist culture. Distance Covered: 120-130 Kms Travel Time: 04-06 hrs Cab Meal: Breakfast and Dinner

DAY 4: Kaza - Langza - Komik - Hikkim - Kaza

Today morning, get ready for the Tour - Langza, Hikkim, Komic. Later on you can explore the Kaza Market (On Your own). After Exploring the market come back to the Stay. Dinner and overnight stay at Kaza.Langza, Is famous for its fossil hunting. Due to its close proximity to the China border, most of the population in Langza practice Tibetan Buddhism. It is an interesting place for handicraft lovers as the primary occupation of the villagers is mud craft. Vessels, ornaments, and souvenirs of all shapes and sizes are produced in this village and sold all across the country. Hikkim, The world’s highest post office in the world. If possible, you might even meet the postmaster and hear his stories and have the experience to send a postcard to your family, love one from the nominated Post Office. Komic, Is the world's highest connecting Village with the motorable road which literally translates to ‘eye of a snow cock’, is situated at a height of 4580 meters. This farming village has a population of 84 people, living in utter isolation, cut-off from the rest of the world for most parts of the year. This little hamlet ensures to bring you thousands of miles away from your familiar settings to a place of soft brown pastures and snow-white mountain peaks. Some of the common animals of the region are the Tibetan wolf, blue sheep, red fox, hare, snow cock, Himalayan griffin, rock pigeon etc.
Distance Covered: 45-60 Kms Travel Time: 03-05 hrs Cab
Meal: Breakfast and Dinner

DAY 5: Kaza - Kii - Kibber - Chicham - Tabo

Today morning, get ready for the Tour - Key Monastery, Kibber, Chicham Bridge. Later on move towards Tabo. On Arrival at Tabo, Check-in to the Stay and retire for the day. Dinner and Overnight stay at Tabo. Key Monastery, Founded more than a 1000 years ago by Dromton, Key Gompa Monastery located in the Lahaul and Spiti district of Himachal Pradesh. Key Monastery or Kye Gompa is an absolutely transcendent place of worship which is now officially the biggest monastery of Spiti Valley and a religious training centre for Lamas. Kibber, is the best place for travelers in the entire Spiti Valley. Located at a dizzying altitude of 4200m above sea level, Kibber village is famous for a monastery and the Kibber Wildlife Sanctuary Chicham Bridge, Located at a height of around 4500 meters above sea level in the Himalayan mountainranges, the Chicham Bridge is said to be the world's highest suspension bridge and is one of the most spectacular bridges of the world. The bridge is situated across a deep gorge around 1000 feet in height. The bridge connects Kibber village with the Chicham village in the Spiti valley and is frequently used by motorists for approaching the Kunzum Pass. The bridge was opened to the public in the year 2017.
Distance Covered: 90-110 Kms Travel Time: 04-06 hrs Cab
Meal: Breakfast and Dinner

DAY 6: Tabo - Nako - Rakcham

Today morning, After Breakfast start your journey for Sangla/Rakcham. We will again cross the Nako Village (From Outer side) which is famous for a beautiful lake. Later continue drive Enroute the best viewing of Satluj & Spiti Rivers at Khab and Karcham Bridge. On Arrival at Sangla/Rakcham, Visit - Sangla, Rakcham and Chitkul Village (Last Village on the old Indo Tibetan Trade Route). Also enjoy the great views of Baspa River and Valley. Later on Check-in to the stay and retire for the day. Dinner and Overnight stay at Sangla/Rakcham.
Distance Covered: 205-220 Kms Travel Time: 07-08 hrs Cab
Meal: Breakfast and Dinner

DAY 7: Rakcham - Shimla (Drop/Departure)

Today morning, After Breakfast Check-out from the Stay and Move for Shimla. On the way, enjoy the Outer view of the beautiful Karcham, Wangtu, Rampur and Kufri. On Arrival at Shimla, Our Driver will drop you at Shimla Bus Stand and from there onwards you can move for your onward Destination on your own. You will remember this unexplored Kinnaur and Spiti Trip till the lifetime with the beautiful memories- The Unexplored Hidden Gem of India. Tour Ends.
Distance Covered: 250-280 Kms Travel Time: 09-10 hrs Cab Meal: Breakfast
