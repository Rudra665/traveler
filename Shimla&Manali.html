Shimla & Minali

Shimla and Manali, nestled in the picturesque state of Himachal Pradesh in northern India, are two of the most enchanting and sought-after tourist destinations in the country. Shimla, the capital city of Himachal Pradesh, is renowned for its colonial charm and breathtaking landscapes. The Mall Road, with its vibrant shops and colonial architecture, is a bustling hub for tourists. The Ridge offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains, and Jakhoo Temple provides a serene escape. Visitors can also experience the toy train journey on the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Kalka-Shimla Railway, winding through hills and tunnels, offering a nostalgic journey into the past.
Manali, on the other hand, is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Surrounded by towering snow-capped peaks and lush green valleys, Manali is a gateway to the mesmerizing Rohtang Pass, where visitors can revel in snow activities and behold the stunning landscapes. The Beas River flows through the town, providing opportunities for river rafting and trout fishing. Old Manali exudes a bohemian charm with its vibrant cafes, artistic alleys, and the ancient Manu Temple. The Solang Valley, known for its ski slopes and paragliding, is another highlight for those seeking an adrenaline rush.
Both Shimla and Manali offer a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and adventure. Whether it's the colonial architecture of Shimla or the Himalayan grandeur of Manali, these destinations captivate the hearts of tourists, making them unforgettable experiences in the lap of the Himalayas.

Himachal Pradesh is a magical place where the sun sets in paradise. Reorganized as the 18th Indian state in 1971, Himachal is located in the northwestern region of the Himalaya. Himachal is landlocked between Jammu and Kashmir to the north, Tibetan plateau to the west, and Punjab to the east; the state stands apart in terms of its sheer topographic diversity and breath taking pristine natural beauty. It is said that the land possesses such magnetism that it attracts nature lovers and voyagers from not only India but across the globe

Quick Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Shimla

Arrival in Shimla and check-in to the hotel. Explore the nearby attractions like The Mall Road, Jakhu Temple, and Ridge. Overnight stay in Shimla.

DAY 2: Visit Kurfi

Visit Kufri, a popular tourist spot known for its scenic beauty, and enjoy adventure sports like skiing, horse riding, etc. In the evening, visit the Christ Church and Shimla State Museum. Overnight stay in Shimla.

DAY 3: shimla- manali

Drive from Shimla to Manali, which takes about 8-9 hours. On the way, visit the Pandoh Dam and Hanogi Temple. Check-in to the hotel and relax. Overnight stay in Manali.

DAY 4: Manali local sightseeing

Visit the famous attractions in Manali like the Hadimba Temple, Vashisht Hot Springs, and Manu Temple. In the evening, explore the local market and indulge in shopping. Overnight stay in Manali.

DAY 5: Solang Valley- Atal Tunnel

Visit the Solang Valley and indulge in adventure sports like paragliding, zorbing, and horse riding and then visit Atal Tunnel . In the evening, take a walk around the Mall Road and indulge in some last-minute shopping. Overnight stay in Manali.

DAY 6: Shimla - Delhi

Departure from Manali and drive back Delhi.
