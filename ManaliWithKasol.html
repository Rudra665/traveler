Manali

with Kasol

Nestled in the scenic Himalayan mountains of Himachal Pradesh, Manali and Kasol stand out as two charming destinations that captivate travelers with their natural beauty and distinct vibes. Manali, often referred to as the "Valley of the Gods," is a popular hill station known for its breathtaking landscapes, including snow-capped peaks, lush green valleys, and the meandering Beas River. The town offers a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility, with opportunities for activities like skiing, paragliding, and trekking, as well as serene moments by the riverside or exploring the vibrant markets.
Just a few hours away from Manali, Kasol is a hidden gem in the Parvati Valley. Known for its laid-back atmosphere and pristine surroundings, Kasol has become a haven for backpackers and nature enthusiasts. The Parvati River flows alongside the village, adding to the picturesque setting. Kasol is celebrated for its diverse cultural scene, with a mix of Israeli, Indian, and international influences reflected in its cafes, music festivals, and vibrant street art. The trek to Kheerganga, starting from Kasol, is a popular adventure that rewards trekkers with hot springs and panoramic mountain views.
Combining a visit to Manali and Kasol in one trip provides a delightful contrast. While Manali offers a more bustling and commercialized experience with a range of activities, Kasol beckons those seeking a more laid-back and introspective journey. Travelers can revel in the scenic drives between these two destinations, taking in the changing landscapes and experiencing the unique charm each place has to offer, making for an unforgettable exploration of Himachal Pradesh's mountainous beauty.

Overview

A land of splendid mountains, a pristine place which feeds and provides solace to the mind and soul, a pious land which is associated with the supernatural, an enigma of a place which attracts people from every nook and corner of the world- It's incredible, it's beautiful, its enchanting- It's Himachal! Himachal Pradesh is a magical place where the sun sets in paradise. Reorganized as the 18th Indian state in 1971, Himachal is located on the north-western region of the Himalaya. Himachal is landlocked between Jammu and Kashmir to the north, Tibetan plateau to the west, and Punjab to the east; the state stands apart in terms of its sheer topographic diversity and breath taking pristine natural beauty. It is said that the land possesses such magnetism that it attracts nature lovers and voyagers from not only India but across the globe.

Quick Itinerary

Day 1: Delhi to Manali

Our representative will meet you and provide instructions ,then transfer you to Manali bus boarding point from there by Volvo (an overnight bus journey).

DAY 2: Full Day Sight Seeing

Reach Manali by morning, check-Into hotel and relax a bit before exploring Manali. Manali, is a Hill station with year-round cool Climate and lots of snow in the winter. Check in to the hotel and go for local sightseeing through the streets of Manali. You can visit Hadimba temple, Manu temple & spend some time roaming old Manali cafes Mall road & the Manali Sanctuary. Overnight stay at hotel

DAY 3: Solang Valley

After a delicious breakfast, the group will be transferred towards Solang valley or Gulaba. The place is the main adventure hub, and you can enjoy the snow activities in the winter. Activities mainly include Paragliding and Zorbing. After visiting Solang or Gulaba we move towards Vashisht by evening. Enjoy the local Manali village and temple which also have a hot water springs and further up the hill there isthe Jogini waterfall which is a short trek from Vashisht. Overnight stay at Manali.

DAY 4: Manali to kasol via naggar

Today is the end of your amazing experience in Manali Morning after breakfast check out from hotel transfer towards Kasol, visit Naggar village on the en route. You have the option for rafting in Kullu. Kasol is a proper hippie village for those who backpack across India. The village is also a major hub for some popular treks across the Parvati valley. Overnight stay at kasol

DAY 5: Hike to chalal village

Have a fulfilling breakfast from the home stay and the tour will commence with a small hike to the nearby village of Chalal. The hike is for you to get acclimatized to manner and ways of the Himalayan villages. After the trek return to kasol and overnight transfer to Delhi.

DAY 6: Day to perform adventure

Reach Delhi early in the morning taking home cherishing memories from the trip.
